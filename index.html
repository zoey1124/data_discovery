Project AEI

Using A.I. and Real-Time Data for the Development of an early warning system for public safety and the recognition of emotions.

The main objective of this project is to use emotion recognition software to both predict physical behaviors and to create a mindfulness platform.

Introduction

Human beings have a rather unique ability to discern the emotional states of one another via their unconscious perception, processing, and identification of the specific acoustic features associated with particular emotions.

This project aims to use a neural network to identify the acoustic features associated with the positive and negative emotions of police officers, citizens, and general individuals to determine whether one could use vocal cues and real-time acoustic feature analysis to predict violent behavior, on the part of police officers towards citizens, before it happens.

However, the uses of the speech emotion recognition software that we are developing can extend beyond its potential use as a tool to predict violence, it also has the potential to serve as the backbone for services that can enhance the human experience, and allow individuals to live happier and healthier lives.

With this in mind, this project also assesses how a tool that allows people to have a more comprehensive understanding of their emotional states can and will impact the delivery and effectiveness of both mindfulness resources, and a given persons experience using any particular service or device.

Project AEI aims to close the gap between man and machine through the development of artificial emotional intelligence, a tool that will allow artificial intelligence to understand the emotions of the humans they interact with.

Data Sets

The datasets used for the training of our high accuracy neural network consist of audio files sourced from datasets like TESS, that we organized and annotated in accordance with the emotions that are universal across the face, voice, and brain.
The list of said emotions comes from our consultation with a psychologist who is renowned both across the nation and the world for their work in the study of happiness and emotions, as well as our own personal review of various papers in the fields that concern our work.
